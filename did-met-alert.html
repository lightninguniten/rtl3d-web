DID & MET — Early warning & alert systems

The Department of Irrigation and Drainage (DID) and the Malaysian Meteorological Department (MET) are key government partners for translating RTL3D nowcasts into actionable alerts for water management and public weather safety.

Department of Irrigation and Drainage (DID)

DID manages dams, rivers, and drainage infrastructure across Malaysia. Lightning and intense rainfall can threaten dam safety, trigger flash floods, and disrupt water supply operations. Observation stations at Batu Dam and Padang Jawa anchor hydrological monitoring within the RTL3D study network.

Malaysian Meteorological Department (MET)

MET issues official weather forecasts and severe thunderstorm warnings for aviation, maritime traffic, agriculture, and the public. The MET Kuala Pilah site is the network origin for LF localization, linking national forecasting needs to high-resolution lightning imaging in the Straits of Malacca region.

How RTL3D supports DID & MET

Real-time 3D charge imaging and CG lightning nowcasting provide lead time before strikes and intense electrification affect exposed assets and populated catchments. Joint workshops connect scientific products to operational alert workflows.

  • Dam and catchment early-warning support
  • Thunderstorm nowcasts for MET warning products
  • IoT countermeasures and field alert trials
  • Shared visualization for agency briefings

Importance of this system

Flood and severe-weather response depends on knowing where lightning activity is intensifying relative to dams, rivers, and communities. RTL3D maps localized sources and 15 km radiation zones so DID and MET teams can coordinate warnings, reservoir operations, and public advisories across the study region.
