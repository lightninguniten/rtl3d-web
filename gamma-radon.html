Role in RTL3D

Three monitor sites record gamma dose rate and energy spectra during storms across the Straits of Malacca network. Data complement electric-field mills and LF/VHF imaging when validating thundercloud electrification.

What is observed?

Thunderstorm ground enhancements (TGE) — short-lived gamma-ray bursts from relativistic electrons. Radon progeny radiation — longer-lived low-energy gamma from uplifted aerosols in strong near-surface fields.

Why it matters

Enhanced electron and photon fluxes in thunderstorm electric fields may contribute to air ionization, conductivity growth, and lightning initiation — a key research thread from Japan’s winter-thunderstorm studies to tropical Malaysia.

Key research & references

  • Torii, Takeishi & Hosono (2002) — gamma-ray dose increases up to ~0.1 mGy during winter thunderstorms in Japan; continuous spectrum to several MeV consistent with bremsstrahlung from energetic electrons (J. Geophys. Res., doi:10.1029/2001JD000938).
  • Torii et al. (2005) — Monte Carlo study showing electron and photon fluxes increase greatly where field strength exceeds ~280 P(z) kV/m; cosmic-ray muon secondary electrons are a key source; radon-progeny beta/gamma also discussed.
  • TGE bursts often precede or coincide with lightning; a return stroke can abruptly terminate the high-energy component while radon-derived low-energy gamma may persist for hours after the near-surface field relaxes.

Physical processes

Winter TGE discovery

1 / 4

Torii, Takeishi & Hosono (2002) documented transient gamma-ray dose increases during winter thunderstorms on the Sea of Japan coast. NaI pulse-height analysis revealed a continuous spectrum to several MeV — consistent with bremsstrahlung from relativistic electrons accelerated in thundercloud fields.

Monte Carlo simulations (Torii et al. 2005) show MeV electron and photon fluxes rise sharply when thunderstorm field strength exceeds ~280 P(z) kV/m. Cosmic-ray muons seed secondary electrons; avalanches produce bremsstrahlung gamma detectable at the surface — often before cloud-to-ground lightning.

Near-surface electric fields lift charged aerosols bearing radon daughters (²¹⁴Pb, t½ ≈ 27 min; ²¹⁴Bi, t½ ≈ 20 min). This adds a lower-energy gamma component that can persist 1–3 hours after the field returns to fair-weather values — distinct from short RREA bursts.

Three gamma/radon monitors in the RTL3D network record TGE events alongside electric-field mills and LF lightning imaging — building a multi-parameter picture of electrification, high-energy particle flux, and eventual discharge across the Straits of Malacca study region.
