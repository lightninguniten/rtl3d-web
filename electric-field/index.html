Role in RTL3D

Six sensor sets measure the surface electric field across Strait of Malacca. Records validate 3D charge imaging and flag electrification before the first strike.

Sensor platform

Inverted electric field mills (EFM-100 class) with OTOWA Electric support for calibration, installation, and site lightning protection.

What is measured?

Vertical field in kV/m. Thundercloud charge separation builds the gradient; a return stroke appears as a sudden step change in the time series.

How it works

Field mill principle

1 / 4

A rotating shutter alternately exposes and shields a ground electrode. Induced charge flow is demodulated into magnitude, polarity, and a 20 Hz time series.

Fair-weather baseline is ~0.1 kV/m. Fields rise through electrification; a return stroke produces a sharp step change. Trends matter more than absolute calibration during active storms.

Configurable thresholds drive All Clear, high-field (yellow), very-high (red), and lightning (purple) states. Inverted mounting reduces false alarms in heavy rain.

Five electric-field sensor sets feed the RTL3D nowcast hub alongside LF, VHF, and 3D charge imaging — validating near-field charge estimates and complementing channel localization.
